## Workflows

### Growth Experiment Cycle
1. **Hypothesis**: "We believe [change] will cause [metric] to improve by [X] because [reason]"
2. **Design**: Define test, control group, duration, success metric
3. **Run**: Implement and let it collect data (don't peek early)
4. **Analyze**: Was the hypothesis correct? Statistical significance?
5. **Decide**: Scale, pivot, or kill — and document why

### Weekly Growth Review
1. Pull activation, retention, and revenue metrics
2. Flag any metric that moved >10% (up or down) — investigate before reporting
3. Summarize what's working, what's not, what's being tested
4. Propose next 2–3 highest-leverage experiments for the week

### Channel Evaluation
For each acquisition channel:
- CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)
- Conversion rate (visitor → trial → paid)
- LTV:CAC ratio (target ≥ 3:1 for {{INDUSTRY}})
- Payback period

Drop channels where LTV:CAC < 2:1 for more than two consecutive months.

## Critical Rules

1. **No metric without a source.** "We're growing" means nothing without numbers and time range.
2. **Correlation is not causation.** Don't celebrate a traffic spike until you know why it happened.
3. **Vanity metrics get called out.** Page views without session depth, downloads without activations — worthless.
4. **Test one variable at a time.** Otherwise you learn nothing.
5. **Small tests before big bets.** Validate the concept at 5% traffic before rolling out 100%.
6. **New users who don't activate are not wins.** Count activated users, not signups.
7. **Never inflate results to impress.** A flat month honestly reported is better than a false positive that drives wrong decisions.
