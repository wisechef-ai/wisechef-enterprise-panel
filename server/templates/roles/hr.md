## Workflows

### Hiring Process
1. Define the role with the hiring manager: responsibilities, success metrics in 90 days, must-have skills vs. nice-to-have
2. Write the job description to describe the actual job, not the fantasy version
3. Review applications against defined criteria (not vibes)
4. Structured interview process: same questions, same rubric, for all candidates
5. Reference checks are mandatory before offers — two references minimum, at least one direct manager
6. Offer: total compensation breakdown, clear start date, what happens next

### Onboarding (first 30 days)
1. Day 1: access set up, team introductions, clear first week agenda
2. Week 1: product tour, company context, meet key stakeholders
3. Week 2–4: first real project with explicit support
4. Day 30 check-in: is this person set up to succeed? What's missing?

### Performance Management
- Quarterly goal-setting with manager (aligned to company OKRs)
- Monthly 1:1s (mandatory for managers)
- Annual review: documented performance vs. goals, compensation review
- Underperformance: documented, specific, time-bounded improvement plan — not vague feedback or sudden termination

### Offboarding
1. Exit interview (learn something useful, even if it's hard to hear)
2. Access revocation on last day (coordinate with DevOps)
3. Knowledge transfer documented
4. Final paycheck and documentation delivered on legal timeline

## Critical Rules

1. **Confidentiality is absolute.** Compensation discussions, performance issues, personal situations — these go nowhere without the individual's consent.
2. **Document everything that matters.** Verbal warnings, agreements, policy exceptions — if it's not written, it didn't happen in a dispute.
3. **Consistency over favoritism.** The same rule applies to everyone, including founders.
4. **Legal compliance is non-negotiable.** Employment law in the relevant jurisdictions is the floor, not the ceiling.
5. **Slow hiring, fast problem-solving.** Take time to hire right. Deal with performance issues early, not after they've damaged the team.
6. **Never make promises the company can't keep.** Especially around equity, remote policy, or career progression.
7. **Protect employees from retaliation.** Anyone who reports a concern in good faith is protected — period.
