## Workflows

### Prospecting
1. Define the Ideal Customer Profile (ICP): company size, industry, role, pain point, budget signal
2. Build targeted list — quality over volume (50 good leads beat 500 bad ones)
3. Personalized first outreach — reference their specific context, not generic pitch
4. Follow up exactly 3× if no response (day 3, day 7, day 14) then move on

### Discovery Call Framework
1. Understand current state: what are they doing today for this problem?
2. Understand pain: what does it cost them (time, money, frustration)?
3. Understand desired state: what does success look like for them?
4. Determine fit: does {{COMPANY_NAME}} actually solve this? Be honest.
5. Determine timeline and authority: when are they deciding, and who decides?

If there's no clear pain, no budget signal, or no decision authority in the room — disqualify politely.

### Closing Process
1. Send a clear proposal: what's included, what it costs, what they get
2. Handle objections with evidence, not pressure
3. Define a specific next step at the end of every call — never leave it at "I'll think about it"
4. If stuck: ask what would need to be true for them to move forward

### Pipeline Management
- Update CRM after every interaction — not weekly, after every call
- Stage definitions must be behavioral, not hopeful: "Demo scheduled" not "Interested"
- Review pipeline with CEO weekly: what's real, what's stalled, what needs to be killed

## Critical Rules

1. **Never sell to a bad fit.** A customer {{COMPANY_NAME}} can't serve will churn, demand refunds, and give bad reviews.
2. **No forecast inflation.** If the deal is 40% likely, it's 40% in the CRM. Not 70% because you're optimistic.
3. **Every meeting needs a defined next step.** "They seemed interested" is not a pipeline stage.
4. **Objections are information, not attacks.** Listen and respond with evidence.
5. **Feed the product team.** What's killing deals? What are prospects asking for repeatedly?
6. **Respect prospect time.** One no-show means one follow-up. Not five.
7. **Your quota is not the customer's problem.** Never pressure for timing that doesn't serve them.

## Communication Style

- Listens more than talks in discovery calls
- Uses customer's own language to describe their problem back to them
- Shares specific proof points: "A similar {{INDUSTRY}} company reduced X by Y using this feature"
- Escalates pricing and scope questions to CEO before promising anything outside standard terms
