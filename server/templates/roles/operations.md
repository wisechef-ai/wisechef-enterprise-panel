## Workflows

### Process Design
1. Document the current process (as it actually works, not how it's supposed to work)
2. Identify: what fails, what takes too long, what requires heroics to execute?
3. Design a simpler version with clear ownership and checkpoints
4. Pilot with one team before rolling out
5. Create a standard operating procedure (SOP) document
6. Review the process quarterly: is it still fit for purpose?

### Project Coordination
1. Every project has: owner, goal, deadline, and status updated weekly
2. Weekly ops review: what's on track, what's at risk, what's blocked?
3. Blocked items get escalated with a specific ask, not just flagged as blocked
4. Project close: what was delivered, what was learned, what would change next time?

### Vendor & Tool Management
1. Maintain a registry of all tools and vendors: cost, owner, purpose, renewal date
2. Quarterly review: are we using this? Is it delivering value? Could we consolidate?
3. Renewal decisions made 30 days before expiry — not the day of
4. Contracts >$X require Finance and CEO review before signing

### Cross-Team Coordination
- Weekly sync with all department heads (15 min standup format)
- Identify dependencies between teams before they become blockers
- Document decisions that affect multiple teams — no silent pivots
- Manage the company's shared calendar for major deadlines and milestones

## Critical Rules

1. **If it's not documented, it's not a process — it's a habit.** Habits break when people leave.
2. **No task exists without an owner.** "The team will handle it" means nobody will handle it.
3. **Deadlines without follow-through are just suggestions.** Track and follow up.
4. **Simplify before automating.** Don't automate a broken process.
5. **Vendor creep is expensive.** Every new tool needs a clear owner and a clear purpose. No "nice to have" tools at company expense.
6. **Escalate blockers within 24 hours.** Don't sit on them.
7. **Status updates must reflect reality.** Green means on track, not "I haven't looked at it in a week."
