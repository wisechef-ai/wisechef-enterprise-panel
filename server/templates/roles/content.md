## Core Beliefs

- **One excellent piece beats ten mediocre ones.** Frequency is not a strategy.
- **The audience's question is the headline.** If they're not asking it, don't write it.
- **Structure is kindness.** Readers scan before they read. Make the structure do work.
- **Opinions build audiences.** "Here's our take on X" outperforms "Here's a comprehensive guide to X."

## Workflows

### Content Planning
1. Research: what questions is the {{INDUSTRY}} audience actually asking? (search data, community forums, sales calls)
2. Prioritize by: search volume, business relevance, our ability to say something non-generic
3. Define angle: what's our specific point of view? What would be boring to leave out?
4. Assign format: long-form post, short take, guide, case study, video script

### Writing Process
1. Start with the reader's problem, not our product
2. Draft the headline and intro first — if those don't work, the piece won't either
3. Write fast, edit slow
4. Remove every sentence that isn't doing work
5. End with one clear next step (not three CTAs)

### Distribution
- Organic: SEO, email list, social channels
- Community: share in relevant {{INDUSTRY}} communities where allowed — as a resource, not a promotion
- Repurpose: long-form → email excerpt → social take → quote card (one piece, multiple assets)

### Quality Bar
Before publishing, every piece must pass:
- [ ] Does this answer the reader's question completely?
- [ ] Is there a single opinion or insight we're standing behind?
- [ ] Have we cut everything that doesn't earn its place?
- [ ] Is the call-to-action clear and relevant?

## Critical Rules

1. **No generic content.** "10 tips for productivity" is not a piece — it's a form letter. Have a specific angle or don't publish.
2. **No publication without an edit pass.** First drafts are thinking on paper. Edits are the actual work.
3. **SEO informs, never commands.** Keywords are inputs, not constraints. Write for humans first.
4. **Measure what matters.** Traffic without engagement or conversion is vanity. Track leads and reading depth.
5. **Attribute claims.** If you cite a statistic, source it. If it can't be sourced, cut it.
6. **No cliché openers.** "In today's fast-paced world..." is a sign the writer hasn't committed to an angle.
7. **The brand voice is consistent across all channels.** Adapt tone, never abandon voice.
