# NEW_ROLES.md — Agency Agents Roles Worth Adding to WiseChef

Roles from the Agency Agents open-source collection that WiseChef doesn't currently offer as standard roles, but should consider adding.

---

## 🚀 Rapid Prototyper

**Description**: Builds functional proof-of-concept prototypes in 1–3 days using high-velocity development tools (Next.js, Supabase, Shadcn, etc.) to validate ideas before full engineering investment.

**Why it matters**: Companies waste months building the wrong thing. A Rapid Prototyper validates core hypotheses cheaply. In startup-speed {{INDUSTRY}} contexts, this role often has higher ROI than a senior engineer.

**WiseChef Category**: Engineering
**Fits alongside**: Engineer agent (Prototyper goes fast and rough; Engineer builds for production)
**Key behavior from Agency Agents**: Speed-first decisions, "build only what tests the hypothesis," includes user feedback collection from day one.

---

## 🧠 Feedback Synthesizer

**Description**: Collects, consolidates, and distills user feedback from all sources (support tickets, community, sales calls, NPS surveys, interviews) into structured insight reports for Product and CEO.

**Why it matters**: Feedback exists in every company. What's rare is the discipline to synthesize it across channels, remove noise, and present actionable patterns. Most teams have insight gaps, not insight shortages.

**WiseChef Category**: Product / Customer Success
**Fits alongside**: Support agent (generates raw feedback), Product agent (consumes insights)
**Key behavior to steal**: "No insight without source attribution." Every claim in a synthesis report has a quoted source, not just a vibe.

---

## 📡 Trend Researcher

**Description**: Monitors industry signals — competitor moves, emerging {{INDUSTRY}} trends, regulatory shifts, academic research, conference topics — and delivers weekly briefings that surface what matters before it's obvious.

**Why it matters**: Strategy agents (CEO, Product) need environmental awareness. Right now that's implicit. A dedicated Trend Researcher makes it explicit and systematic — and prevents the company from being surprised by what was knowable.

**WiseChef Category**: Strategy / Intelligence
**Fits alongside**: CEO agent (primary consumer), Growth agent (secondary consumer for channel trends)
**Key behavior to steal**: Distinguishes "signal" from "noise" explicitly. Refuses to surface trends that are already mainstream knowledge — novelty and early timing are the value.

---

## 🛡️ Brand Guardian

**Description**: Protects and evolves the company's brand identity across all touchpoints — monitors for brand drift, reviews external-facing materials before publication, maintains brand guidelines, and flags inconsistencies.

**Why it matters**: Brand drift is subtle but compounding. As companies grow and more agents/people create content, visual and tonal inconsistencies accumulate and erode trust. The Brand Guardian is the enforcement layer the Design and Content agents currently lack.

**WiseChef Category**: Design / Marketing
**Fits alongside**: Design agent (visual identity) and Content agent (editorial voice)
**Key behavior from Agency Agents**: Audits against the brand standard before approving — never signs off on material that deviates without documented justification.

---

## 🔬 Evidence Collector (QA Variant)

**Description**: A QA specialist role focused specifically on evidence-based quality assessment — refuses to approve or rate anything without screenshots, reproduction steps, or measurable proof. Default stance: "NEEDS WORK." Requires overwhelming evidence to certify "production ready."

**Why it matters**: Standard QA can suffer from over-optimistic reporting ("A+ on first attempt") or vague sign-offs ("looks good"). The Evidence Collector's aggressive skepticism is a forcing function for honest quality culture.

**WiseChef Category**: Quality Assurance (QA)
**Fits alongside or replaces**: QA agent (this is a more opinionated, harder-line version)
**Key behavior from Agency Agents**: "Zero issues found is a red flag — look harder." Screenshots required for every claim. Realistic grades (C+/B-, never A+ on first attempt).

---

## 🔄 Workflow Optimizer

**Description**: Continuously audits internal workflows across all agents, identifies bottlenecks and redundancies, and proposes process improvements with estimated time/cost savings.

**Why it matters**: WiseChef's multi-agent teams will develop inefficiencies over time — redundant handoffs, unclear ownership, manual steps that could be automated. A Workflow Optimizer is the self-improvement mechanism for the team itself.

**WiseChef Category**: Operations
**Fits alongside**: Operations agent (shares territory but is more diagnostic/improvement-focused vs. execution-focused)
**Key behavior to steal**: Always quantifies the cost of the current process before proposing changes. "This saves approximately 3 hours/week per agent." Doesn't optimize for the sake of it — requires a measurable gain.

## Priority Recommendation

For WiseChef's next role expansion:

| Priority | Role | Rationale |
|----------|------|-----------|
| **High** | Feedback Synthesizer | Fills a real gap in the Product ↔ Customer feedback loop |
| **High** | Brand Guardian | Prevents the design drift that happens at scale |
| **Medium** | Rapid Prototyper | High value for startup-stage WiseChef customers |
| **Medium** | Trend Researcher | Adds strategic intelligence layer most companies lack |
| **Low** | Evidence Collector | Good for QA-mature teams; too intense for early-stage |
| **Low** | Workflow Optimizer | Valuable later; premature for companies still figuring out basic ops |
